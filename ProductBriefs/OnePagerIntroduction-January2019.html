One Pager Times are Changing Traditional industries worldwide are undergoing a technological transformation, often referred to as Industry 4.0, smart production, or industrial digitalization. Along with the promising business opportunities this brings, the expanding organizational IT/OT convergence exposes organizations to new cyber risks. Industrial cyber risks can potentially lead to production downtime, loss of intellectual property, physical damage, loss of reputation and even loss of life. These risks have a generic yet unique nature- even the standard procedure of updating and patching vulnerable ICS involves great complexity. As the threat landscape continues to evolve, industries need to embrace a comprehensive and continuous approach to cybersecurity in order to enjoy the benefits of digitalization. This includes ongoing monitoring process and risk prioritization according to the risks’ potential impact and remedy costs. This continuous process proactively governs and reduces cyber risks, hence, allowing better up-time, productivity, safety and new smart production opportunities.
